The Co-Branded Partner Model

How AWNP grows through other organizations' audiences - without spending a dollar on ads

"We don't need to build an audience from scratch. We need to plug into the audiences that already exist - and give those organizations a reason to share."

The Concept

Every organization in the nonprofit ecosystem has an audience of leaders, board members, and professionals who need exactly what Jim Morgan teaches. State associations, university programs, community foundations, leadership networks - they all have email lists, social channels, and events filled with our target audience.

The co-branded partner model turns each of these organizations into a distribution channel for AWNP. Partners get free, high-quality leadership content branded alongside their name. We get access to their audience. Everyone wins.

How It Works

1
Identify Partner Org with aligned audience
2
Co-Brand Pages Their logo + AWNP on episode pages
3
Partner Shares To their audience via email, social, events
4
Capture & Tag Subscribers tagged by partner source

Real-World Scenarios

Scenario 1

Positive Coaching Alliance Partnership

PCA (pca.org) works with coaches, parents, and athletic directors across thousands of youth sports organizations nationwide. Their mission - developing "Better Athletes, Better People" - aligns directly with Morgan's leadership principles around culture, tone at the top, and walking the talk.

PCA shares co-branded episodes with their network of coaches and organizational leaders. Their audience already thinks about culture-building and leadership development daily. Episodes on culture, psychological safety, and collaborative mindset resonate directly.

PCA's national reach + aligned audience + their existing communication channels: a natural first partner that validates the model for every partnership conversation that follows.

Scenario 2

University Program Integration

A graduate nonprofit management program assigns 3 episodes per semester as supplemental listening. 60 students per cohort, 2 cohorts per year. Students share with their organizations.

Direct subscribers from students + ripple effect into their organizations: ~200-400 subscribers per university per year.

Scenario 3

Community Foundation Grantee Resource

A community foundation adds the co-branded podcast to their grantee resource library. 150 active grantees, each with leadership teams of 3-8 people.

Positioned as professional development for grantees: ~300-600 subscribers over 12 months.

The Multiplier Effect

5 active partners, each driving 200-400 subscribers per month, means 1,000-2,000 new community members monthly - without a single dollar in ad spend. And every partner we add increases the total without increasing our production cost, because the content is already made.

The podcast library is produced once. But it can be co-branded and distributed infinitely. Each new partner is pure leverage - their audience, their channels, their credibility, our content.

What We Need From Partners

The ask is intentionally light. We want partners to say yes quickly:

✅ Permission to use their logo on co-branded pages

✅ Share episodes with their audience (email, social, website)

✅ Brief intro to their communications team for coordination

That's it. We handle all production, hosting, page creation, and email capture infrastructure.
