About Positive Coaching Alliance

Co-Presented Partner

Transforming Youth Sports Since 1998

Positive Coaching Alliance was founded on a radical bet: that the adults in youth sports, coaches, parents, league administrators, set the tone for every young athlete's experience. Not the rulebook. Not the training drills. The people at the top.

PCA's Double-Goal Coach framework trains coaches to pursue two goals: winning and, more importantly, teaching life lessons through sports. They've reached millions of young athletes across thousands of partnerships with schools, leagues, and youth sports organizations.

Why this episode matters for PCA: Morgan's "tone at the top" principle is exactly what PCA teaches on the sideline. A coach who screams at referees teaches players that authority is something to fight. A coach who responds with composure teaches players that character holds under pressure. PCA proves Morgan's boardroom insight works everywhere leadership matters.

Frequently Asked Questions

About This Episode

What does "tone at the top" mean for nonprofits?

According to Jim Morgan, the culture of the board is part of the "tone at the top." When board members treat each other and management with courtesy and respect, they set an example the entire staff emulates. Board behavior cascades downhill and sets the ceiling for everything underneath.

How does Positive Coaching Alliance demonstrate tone at the top?

PCA's Double-Goal Coach framework is Morgan's "tone at the top" translated to the sideline. Coaches set the tone for players, league administrators set the tone for coaches, and parents set the tone from the stands. It's the same cascading leadership dynamic Morgan describes in nonprofit organizations.

How can board members improve their organization's culture?

Morgan asks boards to be intentional: be curious, ask hard questions respectfully, disagree without being disagreeable, show up prepared, and follow through. Every staff member is watching and will mirror what they see at the top. Culture is performed, not declared.

Why does board dysfunction affect staff behavior?

Culture flows downhill. If a board tolerates passive aggression, the staff will too. If board members don't show up prepared or don't follow through on commitments, that signals to the entire organization what's actually acceptable. The gap between stated values and modeled behavior is where culture goes to die.
