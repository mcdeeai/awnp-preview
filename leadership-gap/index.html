The Leadership Gap

Why the next generation is not inheriting the nonprofit sector

The mission is still strong. The talent is still here. What is missing is a clear path. If leadership feels too costly, too foggy, or too far away, fewer people will choose it.

Core Framing

Ambition is there. The path is what feels missing.

Many younger nonprofit professionals are walking away because the road up looks foggy, costly, and not worth it. This version should focus on path, preparation, support, and what would make leadership feel reachable again.

What This Campaign Says
Hard Truth
The next generation is pushing back on a version of leadership that costs too much and gives too little back.

This version should feel clear, plainspoken, and hard to misunderstand.

Optimistic Turn
When people can see a future worth stepping into, more of them will step up.

The hopeful turn here is practical. Build better paths, and more leaders will walk them.

Audience Design

Three tracks, one missing ladder

This version is about visibility, access, preparation, and trust. The case is simple: if the path into leadership stays foggy, the gap keeps widening.

Gen Z Track

The mission still matters. The path is what feels too costly.

This track should validate the lived experience of younger nonprofit workers without flattening them. The core message is simple: leadership has to feel reachable and worth the climb.

  • Voice: direct, validating, hopeful
  • Message: the path needs redesign, not denial
  • Channels: short-form video, social, email, audio snippets
Messenger: Brendo should be the face here. Credibility matters.
Boomer / Legacy Leader Track

You built something worth preserving. Now the handoff has to be built with the same care.

This track should feel respectful, peer-level, and constructive. Legacy leaders built the road we have. The issue is that too little of that road leads clearly to the next generation.

  • Voice: seasoned, candid, generous
  • Message: succession is a leadership act, not an administrative task
  • Channels: LinkedIn, newsletter, podcast, partner conversations
Messenger: Jim Morgan is the natural peer voice here. He can challenge without sounding accusatory.
Bridge / Intergenerational Track

The goal is not to choose a generation. The goal is to build a better bridge.

This is the AWNP signature position. It needs enough bite to feel honest, and enough hope to feel useful. It should show why the path faded and how to build it back.

  • Voice: applied wisdom, not both-sides mush
  • Message: continuity comes from redesign
  • Channels: pillar pieces, partner briefs, talks, keynote material
Writing note: the bridge earns trust by being honest about what is no longer sustainable, then showing what stronger leadership could look like.

Release Plan

A campaign that starts with truth and moves toward renewal

The rollout should build from diagnosis to possibility. First explain the handoff problem clearly. Then show what better leadership design could look like.

1Phase
Foundation

Publish the pillar

Establish the core argument: this is a continuity challenge, not just a workforce complaint. Make the case cleanly, credibly, and without blame language.

2Phase
Proof

Launch one cluster post from each track

Show that the message can hold across generations and contexts. This is where the tone gets tested in the real world.

3Phase
Depth

Add audio treatment + Morgan callouts

Bring in the Applied Wisdom texture. Short audio segments, Jim voiceovers, or quote callouts can make the campaign feel grounded in leadership practice instead of only commentary.

4Phase
Expansion

Build the full 16-piece hub

Turn the pillar and cluster grid into an evergreen content system. The end state is not one page. It is a durable library for talks, newsletters, landing pages, and partner materials.
