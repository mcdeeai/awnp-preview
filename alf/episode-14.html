ALF Silicon Valley: Where Collaboration Transforms Communities

Since 1980, the American Leadership Forum has joined and strengthened diverse leaders to serve the common good. The Silicon Valley chapter has trained hundreds of cross-sector leaders who form the backbone of regional civic leadership.

ALF Fellows don't just network. They transform. Through a year-long journey from I to WE to ALL OF US, diverse leaders build the trust and collaborative capacity to tackle challenges no single sector can solve alone.

"Joining and strengthening diverse leaders to serve the common good." The Senior Fellow network represents decades of accumulated trust, activated every time a complex community challenge demands leaders who can work across difference.

Jim Morgan teaches that people stay where they're growing. ALF Silicon Valley proves it at national scale.

About This Episode

What is Jim Morgan's collaborative mindset framework?

Morgan teaches that you should treat your partner's success as equal to your own. When organizations genuinely invest in each other's success, they unlock a level of effectiveness that competition can never reach.

How does ALF Silicon Valley build collaborative leaders?

ALF's Fellows Program takes proven leaders from different sectors and puts them through a year-long experience designed to build trust and collaborative capacity across difference. Over 4,500 Fellows nationwide have completed the program.

What is the I to WE to ALL OF US journey?

ALF's model moves leaders through three phases: I (individual reflection and growth), WE (building trust and collaboration within the cohort), and ALL OF US (extending collaborative capacity outward to serve the community).

What makes the Senior Fellow network valuable?

When Fellows complete the program, they enter a network of hundreds of leaders who share a common experience and commitment to collaborative leadership. These relationships activate organically when community challenges require cross-sector collaboration.

Who is Jim Morgan?

Jim Morgan transformed Applied Materials into a global technology leader, then dedicated himself to the nonprofit sector. His book Applied Wisdom for the Nonprofit Sector distills decades of leadership into 66 practical concepts. Free at appliedwisdomfornonprofits.org.
