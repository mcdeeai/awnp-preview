Who They Are

The American Leadership Forum, Silicon Valley chapter, is a 501(c)(3) that brings together leaders across sectors for deep dialogue and collaborative leadership development. Their current cohort is Class XLV.

ALF is known for its rigorous, transformative fellowship experience. Senior Fellows span corporate, nonprofit, government, and community sectors. The unifying thread: a commitment to servant leadership and collaborative problem-solving.

  • Model: Dialogue-based leadership development
  • Framework: I to WE to ALL OF US -- individual leadership to collective action
  • Erica Yew is the new CEO. Fresh leadership, open to new partnerships.
  • Chapters nationally: Houston, Oregon, Sacramento, and more
Your Warm Intro Path

You Have Home-Field Advantage

This is not a cold call. You are an ALF SV member. You know the culture, the language, the values. That is your opening.

  • Kathleen is a member -- introduce this as something you're personally bringing to the organization because you believe it fits.
  • Jim Morgan knows ALF. If a direct connection to Erica Yew or senior staff can be made through Jim, that is the gold-standard path.
  • Peer outreach: Reach out to a fellow Senior Fellow you're close with and ask if they'd be willing to make the intro to Erica or the communications team.
  • Frame it as a member initiative: "I found something I think would be valuable for fellows and wanted to bring it to the org." That's not a sales call. That's a contribution.

Outreach to ALF SV Leadership

To Erica Yew, CEO (or your ALF contact)
Subject A weekly leadership resource for ALF fellows
Tone Warm, member-to-organization
Hi Erica, I'm Kathleen [last name], an ALF SV member and a big believer in what this organization builds in people. I'm reaching out because I'm part of the team behind Applied Wisdom for the Nonprofit Sector -- a leadership series built on Jim Morgan's 40+ years of experience, including his transformative work at Applied Materials and the nonprofit sector. I think ALF fellows would love it, and I'd like to offer a simple partnership: We'll deliver a new co-branded leadership episode to your audience every week. You just share it. We handle everything. Each episode is practical, 20-30 minutes, and maps directly to the leadership themes ALF fellows explore in the program. Episode 14, "Collaborative Mindset," was practically written for ALF -- it traces the journey from individual leadership to collective action, which is exactly the I to WE to ALL OF US arc your program is built on. There's no cost, no contract, and no production work on your side. We'd co-brand each episode with ALF SV and deliver it ready to share in your newsletter, member portal, or wherever it would be most useful. I'd love to show you a sample episode on a 20-minute call. Would [day] or [day] work? Warmly, Kathleen [last name] Applied Wisdom for the Nonprofit Sector appliedwisdomfornonprofits.org

Ongoing Partnership

What a Yes Looks Like for ALF

Once ALF SV says yes, the partnership runs itself. Brendan and team deliver a co-branded episode each week. ALF includes it in their communications. Fellows receive world-class leadership content with ALF's name on it, at no cost.

  • All 66 episodes have been mapped to leadership themes that align with ALF's mission
  • Episodes cover culture, planning, execution, communication, trust, and collaboration
  • ALF SV becomes the pilot for national chapter expansion: Houston, Oregon, Sacramento, and more
  • If SV proves the model, 5x the reach is available through other ALF chapters alone
  • Quarterly check-ins to review which episodes resonate most and align with the current class's journey
