Partner Intel

Who They Are

City Year is a national AmeriCorps organization founded in 1988. Their model is "Whole School, Whole Child" -- near-peer mentors placed in high-need schools to provide academic and social-emotional support to students at risk of dropping out.

What makes City Year remarkable isn't just what they do for students. It's what they do for the people who show up to do it. Corps members are 17-25 year olds committing to one of the hardest jobs in education. City Year keeps them through a demanding year not by paying them more -- the stipend is modest -- but by investing relentlessly in who they're becoming.

  • Model: "Whole School, Whole Child" -- near-peer mentors in high-need schools
  • Scale: 2,000+ AmeriCorps members/year, 250+ partner schools across 29 cities
  • Alumni: 40,000+ City Year alumni in the workforce, many in education and social impact
  • Current CTA: Recruiting AmeriCorps members for the 2026-2027 service year
  • National organization with strong city-level leadership networks
Why This Partnership Works

City Year IS Morgan's Framework in Action

Jim Morgan teaches that developing people is the highest-leverage investment a leader can make. City Year has built their entire organization on that exact belief.

Corps members stay through an incredibly demanding year not because of the stipend -- they stay because City Year's culture invests in who they're becoming. Leadership training, mentorship, reflection, community. That's Morgan's "invest in people's growth" framework at operational scale, across 29 cities, year after year.

  • Jim Morgan knows City Year's leadership. This is a warm entry, not a cold call.
  • AWNP as a co-brand is a natural bridge: Leadership content for a corps of 2,000 developing leaders is a perfect fit.
  • Alumni audience: 40,000+ City Year alumni are now in leadership roles across education, nonprofit, and beyond. A long-tail audience for the podcast.
  • Current recruitment angle: Ep 17 directly addresses the "why do corps members stay?" question that City Year needs to answer for prospective recruits.
