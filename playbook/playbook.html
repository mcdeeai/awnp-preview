1Phase
Before You Reach Out
Prepare

Walk in informed. Spend 30 minutes with the partner's page before any outreach.

  • Review the hook episode for this partner (see each partner page for the specific ep). Know the key ideas cold.
  • Understand the value prop from their perspective: what problem does this solve for them? (Hint: weekly content without any production effort.)
  • Know their audience. ALF = senior fellows and leaders. PCA = coaches and admins. CalNonprofits = member orgs. Tailor your language.
  • Review Brendan and team's production assets so you can speak to the co-branding process confidently.
  • Check the landing page at appliedwisdomfornonprofits.org so you can demo it in real time.

The mental model: You are not selling them something. You are bringing them a gift. A weekly gift of thought leadership content, delivered, co-branded, and ready to share. Your job is to make sure they understand what they're saying yes to.

2Phase
Who to Contact
Identify

Find the right person. The wrong contact is wasted energy. You're looking for the person who owns content, communications, or member value.

  • ALF Silicon Valley: Erica Yew is the new CEO. You know this org from the inside as a member. Jim Morgan has existing relationships. Start with a warm intro.
  • PCA: Look for Director of Content, VP of Communications, or the Director of Coach Education. Their team runs programs in all 50 states.
  • CalNonprofits: Focus on the Member Engagement or Communications team. They curate member benefits and newsletter content regularly.
  • Warm intro paths: Always prefer a warm intro over cold outreach. Ask Jim Morgan if he has a connection. LinkedIn second-degree check. Your own ALF network.
  • Decision signal: You want someone with enough authority to say "yes, let's include this in our communications" without committee approval.
3Phase
The Ask
Pitch

Lead with the one-liner. Let it land. Then fill in the details only if they ask.

The one-liner: "We'll deliver a new co-branded leadership episode to your audience every week. You just share it. We handle everything."

Can I show you a sample?"
4Phase
Show, Don't Tell
Demo

The demo is your most powerful moment. A live look at a co-branded episode turns a "maybe" into a "yes."

  • Share the landing page link: appliedwisdomfornonprofits.org - walk them through the episode format, the key takeaways, the production quality.
  • Show the hook episode for their org (see each partner page). It's the one most directly tied to their mission. Start there.
  • Point to the co-branding: Show them where their logo would appear, how the intro would reference their organization.
  • Keep it under 10 minutes. The goal is "I can see exactly how this works." Not an exhaustive tour.

Best line during demo: "Imagine your [fellows / coaches / members] getting something this useful with your name on it every week. And you didn't have to produce a word of it."

5Phase
What Yes Looks Like
Close

Make the yes as easy as possible. Be very clear about what they are agreeing to.

  • What they commit to: Share one co-branded episode per week via their existing channels (email newsletter, social, member portal, etc.).
  • What we provide: The episode, the co-branded assets, the copy, all production. Delivered to them, ready to post.
  • The ask is minimal: Forward an email. Post a link. Add a section to their newsletter. That's it.
  • Start small if needed: "Let's run a 4-week pilot. If your audience loves it, we keep going." Remove any hesitation about long-term commitment.

The close line: "All I need from you is a contact on your comms team and the best way to deliver assets to them. We take it from there."

6Phase
After Yes
Follow Up

A partner who says yes needs to feel supported and glad they did. This is where the relationship compounds.

  • Week 1: Send the first co-branded episode package within 48 hours of agreement. Make the first delivery outstanding.
  • Week 2: Check in briefly. "How did your audience respond? Any feedback?" Show you care.
  • Month 1: Share any subscriber or engagement data you have from their channel. Make the value visible.
  • Month 3: Revisit: "We've delivered 12 episodes. Want to review what's resonating and think about whether other chapters or programs would benefit?"
  • Ongoing cadence: Quarterly check-in, annual review of which episodes mapped best to their programming calendar.
  • For ALF specifically: Success with the SV chapter opens the door to Houston, Oregon, Sacramento, and national chapters. Flag this at the 3-month check-in.
  • Reporting: Even simple metrics (how many times the episode was forwarded, click rates) go a long way in reinforcing the partnership value.
